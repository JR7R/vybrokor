# Vybrokor — Public Release Documentation v1

## 1. Opening Statement

Vybrokor is an open mechanical approach to cleaning the inside of motorcycle fuel tanks.

It is designed to reduce reliance on harsh chemical cleaning methods by using controlled vibration and reusable media instead.

This project is being released publicly to encourage adoption, experimentation, and improvement.

---

## 2. The Problem

Motorcycle fuel tank restoration commonly relies on chemical processes.

These often include:

- acids
- rust removers
- degreasers
- internal tank liners

In many cases, these processes are carried out in home garages or small workshops without structured disposal systems.

As a result, contaminated liquids can end up in:

- household drains  
- driveways  
- soil  
- stormwater systems  

From there, they can make their way into local waterways and eventually the ocean.

This is not always due to negligence — often it is simply a lack of accessible alternatives.

---

## 3. The Vybrokor Concept

Vybrokor takes a different approach.

Instead of relying on chemical reactions, it uses **mechanical action**.

At a high level:

- the fuel tank is securely mounted  
- reusable media is placed inside the tank  
- controlled vibration is applied  
- internal surfaces are cleaned through physical interaction  

The goal is simple:

**reduce or eliminate the need for chemical cleaning wherever possible**

This approach is intended to be repeatable, accessible, and adaptable.
